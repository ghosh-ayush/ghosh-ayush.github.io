Team Promotion & Talent Scaling as a Product Problem

Built a repeatable promotion and talent-scaling system to reduce attrition risk while increasing delivery capacity.

Case StudyPeople & Product LeadershipLeadership Case Study

Context

People & Product Leadership | Leadership Case Study

Problem

As teams scaled, promotion decisions were inconsistent and capacity bottlenecks increased attrition and knowledge-loss risk.

Decision options

  • Fast-track promotions ad hoc to close immediate gaps quickly.
  • Freeze promotions until organization structure stabilizes.
  • Use a structured promotion rubric tied to business need and role readiness.

Chosen decision

Used a structured promotion rubric tied to business need and role readiness.

Tradeoff

Chose rigor and consistency over speed in promotion cycles to avoid short-term morale spikes and long-term capability gaps.

Outcome

  • Reduced attrition and protected continuity on high-priority initiatives.
  • Improved promotion transparency and manager alignment.
  • Increased delivery predictability by aligning people plans with roadmap demand.

Metric

Lower attrition risk and improved delivery predictability via structured promotion and capacity planning.

What I'd do differently

Run calibration workshops one cycle earlier and publish example scorecards sooner to improve manager consistency in the first rollout.
