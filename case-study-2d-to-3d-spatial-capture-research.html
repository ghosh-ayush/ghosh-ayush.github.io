Case Study

Moving from 2D to 3D: Spatial Capture Research

Designed a research roadmap for spatial capture to improve data fidelity while balancing user capture friction.

Case StudyComputer Vision Product ResearchCapture UX Case Study

Context

Computer Vision Product Research | Capture UX Case Study

Problem

2D scalp photos lacked depth, scale, and lighting consistency, producing noisy inputs and unstable model predictions.

Decision options

  • Remain fully 2D to keep capture friction minimal.
  • Move all users to mandatory 3D capture immediately.
  • Adopt a hybrid flow that escalates from 2D to 3D for ambiguous cases.

Chosen decision

Adopted a hybrid flow that escalates from 2D to 3D for ambiguous cases.

Tradeoff

Avoided immediate full 3D rollout to prevent user drop-off while validating quality benefits first.

Outcome

  • Improved clarity on when 3D capture materially improves prediction reliability.
  • Created decision criteria for introducing advanced capture only where value exceeds friction.
  • Provided a roadmap to move toward clinical-grade capture with controlled UX complexity.

Metric

Created decision thresholds for when 3D capture accuracy gains justify additional user effort.

What I'd do differently

Pilot guided capture prompts and real-time quality feedback earlier to reduce user drop-off in advanced capture steps.
